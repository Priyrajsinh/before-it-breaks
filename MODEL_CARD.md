# Model Card — Before It Breaks · Predictive Maintenance (RUL)

A model card following the structure of Mitchell et al. (2019), *Model Cards for Model Reporting*.

## Model details
- **Name:** LSTM Remaining Useful Life Forecaster
- **Owner:** Priyrajsinh Parmar · [github.com/Priyrajsinh](https://github.com/Priyrajsinh)
- **Repository:** https://github.com/Priyrajsinh/before-it-breaks
- **License:** MIT
- **Model type:** Supervised sequence regression (Remaining Useful Life in operational cycles)
- **Architecture:** 2-layer **LSTM**, `hidden_size = 128`, `dropout = 0.2`, batch-first, followed by a linear head to a single RUL scalar.
- **Input:** a sliding window of shape **`[30, 17]`** — the last 30 operational cycles × 17 features (3 operational settings + 14 sensors, after dropping 7 constant sensors).
- **Label:** **piecewise-linear RUL capped at `max_rul = 125`** (Zheng et al. 2017) — raw `max_cycle − current_cycle` is clipped at 125.
- **Training:** Adam (`lr = 1e-3`), batch size 256, up to 50 epochs with **early stopping** (patience 10), **gradient clipping** (max-norm 1.0), and **ReduceLROnPlateau** (patience 5, factor 0.5). Seed 42.
- **Preprocessing:** `StandardScaler` **fitted on training data only** (`models/scaler.pkl`); inference must `scaler.transform()` the window before the forward pass.
- **Framework:** PyTorch. Weights saved as a `state_dict` → `models/lstm_rul.pt` (rule C47).
- **Explainability:** SHAP **`GradientExplainer`** with a 100-window training background (rule C50).

## Intended use
- **Primary use:** predict Remaining Useful Life for industrial turbofan engines and similar continuously-monitored rotating machinery, and surface the single strongest sensor warning signal per prediction.
- **Primary users:** maintenance engineers and reliability teams who schedule preventive maintenance from condition data.
- **Out of scope:** the model is **informational, not autonomous**. It does not ground aircraft, halt turbines, or dispatch crews on its own. A human reads the natural-language recommendation and decides. It is not a safety-of-life controller and must not be wired into one without independent certification.

## Training data
- **Dataset:** NASA CMAPSS **FD001** — 100 train engines, 100 test engines; single operating condition, single fault mode (high-pressure-compressor degradation). Saxena & Goebel (2008).
- **Schema:** see the *Dataset Schema (CMAPSS_SCHEMA — pandera)* section of `CLAUDE.md`. Raw files are pandera-validated **before** any forward pass (rule C34).
- **Features:** 3 operational settings + 14 sensors = **17**, after dropping the 7 near-constant sensors `sensor_1, 5, 6, 10, 16, 18, 19` (rule C40).
- **Split:** **by engine ID** — engines 1–80 train, 81–100 validate (rule C33); the official FD001 test set is the held-out judge. No row-level shuffling (prevents temporal leakage).
- **Lineage / provenance:** raw files are **not committed**; they are DVC-tracked with **SHA-256** sidecars at `data/raw/*.sha256` (rule C41).

## Headline test results
Measured on the official CMAPSS FD001 test set (100 engines) — source `reports/results.json`.

| Metric | Value |
|--------|-------|
| RMSE | **15.79 cycles** |
| MAE | **11.06 cycles** |
| NASA Score (asymmetric, lower is better) | **673.5** |
| Test-set size | 100 engines |

RMSE is comparable to the LSTM baseline of Zheng et al. (2017) (16.14 on FD001). A coverage-regression test fails CI if RMSE exceeds **30 cycles** (rule C37).

## Metrics
- **RMSE / MAE** — standard regression error in cycles.
- **NASA Score** — the official CMAPSS asymmetric penalty: `s_late = exp(err/10) − 1` for late predictions vs `s_early = exp(−err/13) − 1` for early ones. Late forecasts (predicting more life than remains) are penalised harder, encoding the business reality that warning too early is safer than too late.

## Limitations
- **Single fault mode.** FD001 contains only simulated HPC degradation under one operating condition. Generalisation to the multi-condition / multi-fault datasets **FD002–FD004** is **not validated**.
- **Simulator, not field data.** CMAPSS is generated by NASA's C-MAPSS thermodynamic simulator. The **covariate shift between simulator and real engines is not characterised** — the in-repo drift monitor exists precisely to catch this at deployment time.
- **Domain transfer unverified.** The model is trained on aero-engine signatures. Direct transfer to **bearings, gearboxes, or other rotating equipment** is unverified and should be re-trained, not assumed.
- **Right-censored labels.** Test engines stop before failure; ground-truth RUL is the remaining life at the last observed cycle. The 125-cycle cap also means the model does not distinguish "very healthy" from "extremely healthy."

## Ethical considerations & human oversight
- Outputs are **advisory**. The natural-language recommendation is designed for a human-in-the-loop maintenance decision, never autonomous action (no "LSTM"/"SHAP"/"tensor" jargon is exposed to end users — rule C45).
- A wrong prediction has asymmetric cost; the NASA score and the late-prediction penalty bias the model toward earlier (safer) warnings.

## EU AI Act framing
This system falls under **Annex III §2 (Critical infrastructure — management and operation of critical infrastructure)** when deployed in aviation, energy, or transport contexts, and under **Annex III §4 (Employment, workers management and access to self-employment)** when its outputs inform maintenance-worker scheduling. Both categories are **high-risk** under the regulation. The compliance-relevant evidence carried in this repository:

| High-risk requirement | Evidence in this repo |
|-----------------------|-----------------------|
| Documented training-data lineage & governance | pandera schemas on raw + processed data, DVC tracking, SHA-256 sidecars (`data/raw/*.sha256`) |
| Risk management & accuracy monitoring | per-sensor PSI **drift monitor** (alarm at PSI > 0.2) + **coverage-regression CI gate** (RMSE ≤ 30) |
| Human oversight | natural-language, **informational** output — a person schedules the maintenance |
| Transparency & record-keeping | this model card, `reports/results.json`, MLflow run tracking, structured JSON logs |
